    Frequently Asked

    What makes UPlate different?
    UPlate is the only meal prep app built specifically for university students — rank your dining halls, filter by dietary restrictions, and compete with friends on macros, all using real menus and food photos from your own campus.
    Which universities are supported?
    Right now, it's only Purdue, but we're working hard to expand to other major universities. Contact us to request your school!
    Is UPlate free to use?
    Yes! The core app is completely free — macro tracking, dining hall rankings, dietary filters, and social features all come at no cost. UPlate Pro is an optional upgrade for students who want AI-powered recommendations and extra features on top.
    How accurate is the nutrition data?
    We source nutrition data directly from dining halls — no guessing or crowd-sourced estimates.
    Does UPlate use AI?
    AI-powered meal recommendations are available with UPlate Pro. The core app is free and doesn't need AI to be useful — macro tracking, dining hall rankings, dietary filters, and social features all work on their own.
    What dietary restrictions does UPlate support?
    UPlate supports filtering by vegan, vegetarian, gluten-free, nut-free, dairy-free, halal, kosher, and other common restrictions. You can also filter by custom ingredients or sort meals by specific nutritional values like protein, carbs, fat, and calories.
    How is UPlate different from other nutrition apps?
    Unlike general nutrition apps that require manual food entry, UPlate integrates directly with campus dining hall menus. You get accurate nutrition data for the exact meals being served — no searching or estimating required.
    How often are dining hall menus updated?
    Menus are updated in real-time as dining halls publish their daily offerings, so you always see what's currently being served.
